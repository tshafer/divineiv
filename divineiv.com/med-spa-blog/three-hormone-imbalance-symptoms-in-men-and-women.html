Three Hormone Imbalance Symptoms in Men and Women

By: Amy Berkhout, FNP-BC

7/27/2023

Hormonal imbalances are subtle, often masked by symptoms we tend to write off as a "bad day" or "getting older." However, it's crucial to recognize these signals as potentially more significant, requiring medical attention. Hormonal imbalances don't play favorites; they can affect both men and women. Symptoms like weight gain, low libido, and fatigue, often attributed to stress or age, can be indicative of hormonal fluctuations in both women and men. At Divine IV and Wellness in Chandler, AZ, Amy Berkhout, FNP-BC is well-versed in helping individuals recognize and address these signs of imbalance. Below, she will shed light on three common symptoms that might suggest an imbalance in your hormonal health and how hormone replacement therapy can help.

1. Unwanted weight gain

When it comes to hormonal imbalances, one of the first symptoms that men and women often notice is unexplained weight gain. This change isn't your typical "few extra pounds from the holidays." It's weight gain that's resistant to diet and exercise efforts. Hormonal imbalances can affect metabolism and appetite regulation, increasing body fat, particularly around the midsection. It's not about vanity; persistent, unexplained weight gain can be a red flag for health issues and should not be ignored.

2. Low libido

Many individuals experience decreased sexual desire or libido as a symptom of hormonal imbalance. A drop in sex drive can often be linked to fluctuating levels of sex hormones like estrogen and testosterone, impacting both physical arousal and emotional interest in sexual activity. It can strain relationships and lower self-esteem, creating a ripple effect on overall wellness. Understanding that low libido could indicate a hormonal imbalance is the first step toward finding an effective solution.

3. Fatigue

Chronic, unexplained fatigue is another common symptom of hormonal imbalance that is often overlooked. It's not just about feeling tired; it's an overwhelming lack of energy that persists despite adequate sleep. Hormonal imbalances, particularly involving thyroid hormones, adrenal hormones, or insulin, can lead to this pervasive sense of fatigue. It's critical to differentiate between everyday tiredness and fatigue that could signal an underlying hormonal issue.
