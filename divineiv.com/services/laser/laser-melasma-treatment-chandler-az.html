What is Melasma?

Numerous people develop melasma at some time throughout their lives, which occurs when uneven areas of darkened skin show up on the face, forearms, and neck. These tan, gray, or brown patches typically develop evenly around the forehead, cheeks, nose, chin, or upper lip. Melasma develops more often among females with darker skin colors and is thought to be due to sun damage or hormones, like those that occur during menopause and pregnancy. Amy Berkhout, FNP-BC provides melasma treatment at Divine IV and Wellness with Alma Harmony® XL PRO laser technology. This minimally invasive procedure utilizes powerful light energy to help reduce the presence of darkly pigmented areas that are related to melasma. Book an appointment at our Chandler, AZ location to determine whether laser melasma treatment can help boost your self-confidence and enhance your skin tone and appearance.

Laser Melasma Treatment FAQ

What causes melasma?

Melasma can occur during any stage in life. It's commonly believed to be caused by hormonal changes, including those that are experienced with birth control pills and pregnancy. Less prevalent sources that are typically related to melasma include damage from UV rays, stress, and a genetic predisposition.

How does laser treatment for melasma work?

During treatment with the Harmony XL Pro laser, your technician will focus a powerful energy beam into melasmic patches on the surface of your skin. The light will effectively work to break down pigmentation so that it can then be absorbed by your body. Though these areas could become darker initially, they will most likely become less noticeable after about a week.

Does melasma come back after laser treatment?

Though laser options can work to lessen the look of melasma, other gray, brown, or tan areas could develop as time goes on. A series of treatments could be planned to help maintain your results.
